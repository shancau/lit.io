Biography & Research

Kendrick Lamar Duckworth was born on june 17, 1987, in Compton California, to Kenneth “Kenny” Duckworth, a former gang member, and Paula Oliver, a hairdresser. Named after Eddie Kendricks of The Temptations, Lamar grew up in a challenging environment marked by gang violence and poverty. His parents had moved to Compton from Chicago to escape the city’s gang culture, but Lamar still witnessed the harsh realities of street life, including police brutality and murders, which deeply influenced his worldview and later his music.

As a child, Lamar was quiet and introspective, excelling academically despite a stutter. His love for writing was nurtured by his seventh-grade teacher, Mr. Inge, who introduced him to poetry and creative writing. This early exposure to storytelling and metaphors laid the foundation for his future as a lyricist.

Lamar began rapping under the name K-Dot in high school, releasing his first mixtape, Y.H.N.I.C. (Hub City Threat: Minor of the Year), at age 16. His talent caught the attention of Anthony “Top Dawg” Tiffith, who signed him to Top Dawg Entertainment(TDE) in 2005.

In 2011, Lamar released his debut studio album, Section.80, which explored themes of racism, violence and addiction. The album’s critical success led to collaborations with industry heavyweights like Dr. Dre signed him to Aftermath Entertainment and Interscope Records.

Lamar’s major-label debut, good kid, m.A.A.d city (2012) was a concept album that narrated his coming-of-age story in competition. It received widespread acclaim, earning seven grammy nominations. The song/poem I have chosen comes from this specific album and explores the complex relationship between poverty, ambition, and the pursuit of the American Dream in Compton. The song dives deep into the psyche of individuals trapped in a cycle of hardship, where the allure of wealth and the desperation to escape their circumstances intertwine.
